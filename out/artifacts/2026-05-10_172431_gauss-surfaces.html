Gauss surfaces: what counts

Flux is surface accounting. A face contributes when the field pierces it; it contributes zero when the field skims along it.

The invariant

The same rule explains all three cases. For a line charge, the field pierces the curved wall, not the caps. For a sheet, the field pierces the two opposite faces, not the sides. For a conductor, the inside face contributes zero because $E=0$ inside the metal.
